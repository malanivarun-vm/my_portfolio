Andiamo · trip coordination

A product for the part of group travel nobody actually owns

Andiamo is a coordination layer for friend groups planning trips. It is not a booking tool or itinerary builder. It owns the messy phase before that: getting the group aligned on commitment, vibe, budget, destination, and dates.

Product thesis

The real problem is not logistics. It is coordination.

The organiser usually absorbs the planning tax across chats, spreadsheets, and booking tabs. Andiamo reframes the problem: the failure point is commitment and alignment, not “finding enough tools.”

Key decisions

The product is designed around accountability and social pressure

  • Invite preview uses FOMO intentionally before auth instead of a blank entry wall.
  • Private budgets protect honesty while still giving the organiser a usable range.
  • Vote counts are hidden on public preview to avoid bandwagon effects.
  • WhatsApp is treated as infrastructure, not a competitor to replace.
Why it matters

Most group trips die in a group chat. Andiamo owns the gap before that.

Group travel fails at a consistent point: between intent and commitment. The organiser absorbs hours of coordination across chats, spreadsheets, and booking tabs. Members ghost polls. Budget misalignment surfaces after someone has already bought flights. Andiamo is built around the insight that the problem is not tools — it is the absence of structure, accountability, and a private way to surface the awkward questions early. The product does not replace Wanderlog or Splitwise. It owns the decision-making layer they both skip.
