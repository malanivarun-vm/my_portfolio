YouTube · long-form discovery

Long-form content on YouTube is not dying. The feed that surfaces it is.

A PM case study and prototype set addressing YouTube's long-form discovery gap in India's mobile-first market. Built with primary research (171-person survey), full secondary research synthesis, and four live prototypes — each targeting a different leverage point in the same failure loop.

Case study · 4 live prototypes Consumer product Discovery systems India / Mobile
Problem

Users aged 18–34 in India see fewer than 2 relevant long-form learning videos in their first 5 homepage scrolls.

Long-form still accounts for 70%+ of total YouTube watch time. Gen Z has not rejected it — 51% name it as a favourite format. But 47% of users manually search every session because the homepage does not surface it, 29% of Gen Z sessions end without watching anything, and users now spend an average of 12 minutes per session just finding something to watch — up 60% since 2019.

The problem is not content quality. It is feed design. And it is creating a loop: the homepage stops surfacing long videos → users stop finding them → creators see poor early performance → creators shift effort toward Shorts → the homepage has even less good long-form to show.

Research

171-person survey, ecosystem teardown, and 27 external sources — with data gaps called out explicitly.

  • Primary survey of 171 respondents (250–300 distributed). 59% stop watching long videos because the content feels stretched — not because they lost interest in the topic. The interest is there. The content quality is failing them.
  • Only 32% of users ever discover a long video by just opening the app. The remaining majority have to actively search — making the homepage almost irrelevant for long-form discovery.
  • After watching a Short, only 15% of users actively look for a longer video from the same creator. But 41% say they would watch one if it were easy to find. The demand exists. The pathway does not.
  • Secondary research consolidated: market sizing (YouTube $60B+ revenue, 491M India MAU), device shift (TV 36% of US watch hours and growing), creator economics (long-form $3–6 RPM vs Shorts $0.03–0.07), and a competitor graveyard showing every bolted-on cross-format feature has failed within 2 years.
  • Critical unknown: Shorts → long-form session transition rate has never been disclosed by YouTube. The entire funnel thesis of this PRD rests on an assumption that is explicitly labelled as the #1 hypothesis to validate before scaling any solution.
Solutions

Four interventions, each breaking the loop at a different point.

  • Deep Dive Tab — A dedicated feed tab built around declared interests (PM, Finance, Podcasts), ranked by completion rate not view count. No Shorts appear inside it. Deep Picks surfaces quality-ranked content from creators the user does not yet follow — the structural difference from every existing YouTube surface. Open prototype →
  • Shorts-to-Long Bridge — A behaviour-triggered bridge card that appears in the Shorts feed after a user watches 3+ Shorts on the same topic to 95% completion. System-level, cross-creator, intent-matched — not a same-channel link like every existing YouTube mechanism. Open prototype →
  • Long-Form Viewing Streak — A weekly topic-based habit mechanic. Watch 3 qualifying long videos (15 min+, 75% completion) per week per declared category, and the recommendation type evolves: from individual videos → creator playlists → broader category playlists. Framed as personal growth, not platform engagement.
  • Creator Success Suite — AI tools in YouTube Studio (script, editing, captions, SEO) plus a pre-publish Content Quality Score that penalises padding before a video goes live. The only product point where the platform can influence content quality before it damages viewer trust. Open prototype →
Key decisions

What shaped the design choices across all four solutions.

  • No new destinations: The competitor graveyard is unambiguous — IGTV, Netflix Play Something, Netflix Fast Laughs all failed within 2 years. Every solution here lives inside existing surfaces. The Deep Dive Tab is a navigation tab, not an exit from the homepage. The Bridge lives inside the Shorts feed.
  • Completion rate over view count: Deep Picks ranks content by completion rate and rewatch signals, not popularity. High view count is a popularity signal. Completion rate is a quality signal. For long-form these two frequently conflict, and every existing YouTube surface optimises for the wrong one.
  • Explicit assumption labelling: The Shorts → long-form session transition rate is the most commercially important metric in the PRD — and entirely unverified. The solution is designed to test this assumption, not assume it is true. Risk controls are built into the Bridge: if Shorts session length drops more than 5% in A/B testing, bridge card frequency is reduced before any broader rollout.
  • Supply and demand together: Solutions 1–3 address viewer-side discovery friction. Solution 4 addresses creator-side supply incentives. Solving only one side of the market produces a half-fix — creators stop making long videos if discovery stays unpredictable, regardless of how well the feed surfaces them.
