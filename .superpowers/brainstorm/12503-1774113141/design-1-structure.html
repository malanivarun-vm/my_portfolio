Design section 1 of 4: Page structure

The overall flow — what sections exist, in what order, and why.

Proposed page structure (top to bottom)
1
Hero — Who you are + the bold statement
Photo, name, role. Tagline rewritten to be active and problem-framing. No change to layout — just sharper copy.
2
⭐ NEW — Impact Bar
4 large numbers in one accent-coloured strip: 85% adoption · 30% faster go-live · €300K revenue lift · CM +9pp. Credibility at a glance, before they've read a word.
3
What I Do — 3 pillars
Exists already. Copy gets sharper and more specific to your actual working style.
4
⭐ NEW — How I Think (3 principles)
3 sharp product beliefs that reflect your actual working philosophy. Not generic PM wisdom — your specific point of view. This is the section that makes you memorable.
5
Projects — P→A→O expand in place
Exists, but rebuilt with expandable Problem / Approach / Outcome inside each card. The numbers live in the Outcome row. This is where the framework becomes most visible.
6
My Story — tightened
Exists. Gets tighter and more direct — less autobiography, more "what shaped how I think."
7
Contact — unchanged
Already has real email and LinkedIn. Just gets the visual refresh.
The key additions

Two new sections — the Impact Bar (credibility before they scroll) and How I Think (your principles, your POV) — are what turn this from a standard PM portfolio into something that signals a distinct thinker. Everything else is upgrading what already exists.

Does this structure look right? Any section you'd remove, add, or reorder?
