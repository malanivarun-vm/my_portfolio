How bold should the overall design feel?

The content is sorted. Now: how much should the visual design itself signal "this person thinks differently"?

A

Refined and credible — keep the current aesthetic

Clean cards, warm palette, good typography. Uniqueness comes entirely from the content — richer projects, sharper writing, real numbers. The design stays out of the way.

B

Distinctive but professional — a stronger visual identity

Still clean and credible, but with a more specific personality. Think: a strong typographic hierarchy, a bolder use of colour, a structural element that's unmistakably yours — without going "creative portfolio".

C

Unconventional — the design itself is a statement

Layout, interactions, or structure that breaks from the standard PM portfolio template. Higher risk, higher reward — memorable if done well, distracting if not.
